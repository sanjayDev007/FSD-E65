üé≠ Think of Props Like a Theater Performance

Imagine you're directing a play. Each actor (component) needs specific instructions about their role, costume, and lines. Props are like the director's notes - they tell each component exactly what to display, how to behave, and what data to work with. Just like actors can't change their script mid-performance, components can't modify their props - they're read-only instructions from the "director" (parent component)!

üåü What Are Props?

Props (short for "properties") are the way React components receive data from their parent components. They're like function parameters, but for components! Props make components reusable and dynamic.

üí° Key Props Concepts:

  • Read-only: Components cannot modify their props
  • Data flow: Props flow down from parent to child (unidirectional)
  • Any data type: Strings, numbers, objects, arrays, functions, even other components!
  • Reusability: Same component, different props = different behavior
